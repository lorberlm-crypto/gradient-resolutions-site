Reaching out to your sibling
A private worksheet to help you start the conversation

Before you send a message or pick up the phone, this worksheet helps you understand what your sibling may be feeling — and how to reach them in a way that opens a door rather than closing one.

Your answers stay with you. Nothing here is sent to anyone.

Remember: you aren't asking them to commit to a whole process. You're asking for one conversation, with no obligation attached to what happens after it.
Before you write anything

Who should actually deliver this invitation?

Sometimes the message lands better coming from someone other than you — especially if your relationship with this sibling carries old weight. This isn't required. It's worth considering before you draft anything.

The C.H.I.L.D. communication framework
C
CompassionateWritten with care for what your sibling is carrying, not just what you need to say
H
HonestSay what is true without embellishing or omitting what matters
I
IntentionalEvery word earns its place — nothing is vented, nothing is accidental
L
Low-HeatCalm in tone even when the situation isn't — de-escalate, don't trigger
D
DirectClear and firm about what you are asking, without pressure or ultimatum
