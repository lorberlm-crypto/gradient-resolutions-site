What it is

Your Co-Parenting Plan Blueprint is a step-by-step workbook for parents who want to keep their children at the center of every decision — whether you're negotiating a formal co-parenting plan, working through a specific disagreement, or simply trying to get clearer on what you want and how to say it.

It works for parents who are divorcing. For parents who never married. For parents who are still together but struggling to get on the same page. You don't have to be heading into mediation to use it. You don't have to be in crisis.

What it helps you do

  • Get clear on what you actually want for your child — not just your position in the argument
  • Understand how your child experiences the world and what they need to feel secure
  • Prepare for a negotiation — as significant as a custody agreement or as specific as whether to sign your kid up for soccer this fall
  • Communicate more effectively with your co-parent, even when it's hard
  • Build a plan that is tailored to your actual family, not a generic template

The workbook is designed to be used on your own — and it works that way. But you don't have to do it alone. If you're already working with a therapist or coach, they can be a wonderful partner for working through it together.

Why mediation takes longer than you think

Think about the last time someone suggested playing Monopoly on a Saturday afternoon. Did you walk in and start moving pieces immediately? Of course not. Before anyone rolls the dice, you spend real time convincing everyone to play, choosing pieces, reviewing the rules, and figuring out your strategy. The active negotiation is actually the shortest part of the game.

Mediation works the same way. This workbook is the preparation. It's the piece most people skip — and the piece that makes everything else work.

Research shows people are significantly more likely to complete a physical workbook than a digital one — and completion matters. A fillable PDF companion is also available on request once you have your copy.
